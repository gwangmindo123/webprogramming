The Beatles

1960: Formation

The Beatles formed in Liverpool, with the primary lineup of John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr.

1962: "Love Me Do"

The band's first hit, "Love Me Do", was released, marking their breakthrough.

1963: Please Please Me

Their debut album, "Please Please Me", was released.

1964: A Hard Day's Night

The album "A Hard Day's Night" was released, along with a film of the same name.

1965: Rubber Soul

A pivotal album, "Rubber Soul" showed a new level of maturity in the band's songwriting.

1966: Revolver

"Revolver" was released, noted for its studio experimentation.

1967: Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band

A landmark concept album that is one of the most famous and influential albums of all time.

1968: The Beatles (The White Album)

A double album with a wide range of musical styles.

1969: Abbey Road

The last album the band recorded together.

1970: Let It Be

The band's final studio album, released after they had effectively disbanded.

A tribute to the most influential band in history.
